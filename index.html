    🎓 Understanding Air Quality - A Beginner's Guide

    What is AQI (Air Quality Index)?

    AQI is a simple number (0-500) that tells you how clean or polluted the air is. Think of it like a thermometer for air quality!

    0-50: Good - Safe for everyone
    51-100: Moderate - Generally acceptable
    101-150: Unhealthy for sensitive
    151-200: Unhealthy - Avoid outdoors
    201-300: Very unhealthy
    301+: Hazardous - Stay indoors

    Major Pollutants Explained

    • PM2.5 & PM10: Tiny dust particles in the air. PM2.5 is very small and can enter your lungs easily. Main sources: vehicles, construction, industrial smoke.
    • NO₂ (Nitrogen Dioxide): A reddish-brown gas from vehicles and power plants. Can irritate airways and reduce immunity.
    • SO₂ (Sulfur Dioxide): Released from burning coal and oil. Can cause breathing problems and acid rain.
    • CO (Carbon Monoxide): Colorless, odorless gas from incomplete fuel burning. Reduces oxygen supply to organs.
    • O₃ (Ozone): Good in the upper atmosphere, harmful at ground level. Formed by sunlight reacting with pollutants.

    How Does Air Pollution Affect You?

    • Short-term: Eye irritation, coughing, headaches, difficulty breathing, throat irritation
    • Long-term: Asthma, lung diseases, heart problems, reduced lung growth in children
    • Most affected: Children, elderly, pregnant women, people with existing respiratory issues
    • Note: Even healthy people should avoid outdoor exercise when AQI is above 150

    Simple Ways to Protect Yourself

    • ✅ Check AQI daily: Plan outdoor activities when AQI is low (mornings are usually better)
    • 😷 Wear masks: N95/N99 masks filter out 95-99% of particles (normal cloth masks don't help with pollution)
    • 🏠 Indoor air: Use air purifiers, keep plants, avoid smoking indoors
    • 🚗 Reduce exposure: Close car windows in traffic, avoid high-pollution areas
    • 🌱 Help reduce pollution: Use public transport, carpool, save electricity, plant trees

    Understanding Weather Parameters

    • Temperature: Measured in Celsius (°C). Higher temperatures can increase ground-level ozone formation.
    • Humidity: Amount of moisture in air (%). High humidity can make pollutants stick around longer.
    • Wind Speed: Faster winds help disperse pollutants, improving air quality.
    • Pressure: Atmospheric pressure in hPa. Low pressure can trap pollutants near ground.
    • Visibility: How far you can see clearly (km). Reduced by pollution and fog.
    • UV Index: Sun's ultraviolet radiation strength (0-11+). Higher = more sun protection needed.
